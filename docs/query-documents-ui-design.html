A well-drafted non-disclosure agreement (NDA) should include the following key clauses:

  1. Definition of Confidential Information -- Clearly specify what constitutes confidential information, including trade secrets, business plans, and proprietary data.
  2. Obligations of the Receiving Party -- Outline the duty to protect information and restrictions on disclosure to third parties.
  3. Exclusions from Confidentiality -- Define what is not covered, such as publicly available information or independently developed knowledge.
  4. Term and Duration -- Specify how long the NDA remains in effect and whether obligations survive termination.
  5. Remedies for Breach -- Include provisions for injunctive relief and potential damages.
