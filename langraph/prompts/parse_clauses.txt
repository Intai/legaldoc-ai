Examine each section carefully and identify distinct clauses. A clause is a self-contained legal provision that establishes a specific right, obligation, condition, or procedure. Common clause types include but are not limited to:

- Termination: provisions for ending the agreement, including termination for cause and convenience.
- Indemnification: obligations to compensate for losses, damages, or liabilities.
- Confidentiality: restrictions on disclosure of proprietary or sensitive information.
- Limitation of Liability: caps or exclusions on the types or amounts of damages recoverable.
- Non-Compete: restrictions on competitive activities during or after the agreement.
- Non-Solicitation: restrictions on soliciting employees, clients, or customers.
- Intellectual Property: ownership, licensing, or assignment of intellectual property rights.
- Payment: terms governing compensation, invoicing, and payment schedules.
- Warranties: representations and guarantees made by the parties.
- Dispute Resolution: procedures for resolving disagreements, including arbitration and mediation.
- Force Majeure: relief from obligations due to extraordinary events beyond the parties' control.
- Governing Law: the jurisdiction whose laws govern the agreement.
- Severability: treatment of invalid or unenforceable provisions.
- Amendment: procedures for modifying the agreement.
- Notice: requirements for delivering formal communications between parties.
- Assignment: conditions under which rights or obligations may be transferred.
- Entire Agreement: confirmation that the document supersedes prior agreements.
- Waiver: conditions under which a party may waive enforcement of a provision.

When a single section contains multiple distinct clauses, split them into separate entries. When a clause spans multiple subsections that serve a single purpose, combine them into one entry.

For each clause, provide:
- clause_type: a concise label from the list above, or a descriptive label if the clause does not fit a listed type.
- heading: the original section or subsection heading as it appears in the document.
- content: the full text of the clause, preserving its original wording.

Return your output strictly as a JSON array with no additional text, commentary, or markdown formatting:

  {
